Holds an off-the-shelf shampoo, conditioner, or body wash bottle upside-down over a wall-mounted simplehuman pump dispenser so the bottle drains into the dispenser by gravity — no decanting, no funnel, no dedicated refill cap. A six-blade iris diaphragm grips the bottle's neck, so almost any bottle you already own fits.

**Key Features**
- Six-blade iris diaphragm — sliding wedge blades clamp around the bottle neck, fitting a wide range of off-the-shelf shampoo, conditioner, and body wash bottles.
- Snaps onto the top of a wall-mounted simplehuman pump dispenser body (71 mm inner / 76 mm outer).
- Gear-toothed grip rim on the cover and the support — easy to twist with wet hands.
- Captive-tab blades that drop into the plate's pocketed slots and can't lift back out once the cover is on — assembly is tool-free.

**Parts**
Prints as four part types: one support, one plate, one cover, and six blades:
- `support.stl` — the housing that snaps onto the dispenser.
- `diaphragm_plate.stl` — the slotted disc that the blades slide in.
- `blade.stl` — print 6×; each blade is a wedge with a captive tab and a pivot pin.
- `cover.stl` — the front cap with six stadium-shaped pin slots and the gear grip rim.

**Assembly tips**
1. Orient the diaphragm plate so its slots extend counterclockwise when viewed from the top.
2. Slide each blade into a slot and park it at the slot's outer end — the blades overlap when seated, so all six only fit if each is held at the outer edge until the last one is in.
3. Drop the diaphragm plate (with blades loaded) loosely into the support housing — don't push it all the way down yet; leave it floating so the blades can still pivot.
4. Place the cover on top of the blades and adjust each blade so its pin lines up with its stadium slot in the cover.
5. Press the cover down until it snaps onto the support housing, pushing the blades and diaphragm plate into their seated positions.

**Compatibility**
Designed for the simplehuman Wall Mount Pump (444 ml) — slides onto the dispenser body where the cap normally sits. Iris grips bottle necks up to 34 mm in diameter, which covers most full-size shampoo, conditioner, and body wash bottles. Print in PETG if you can — the bathroom is warm and damp, and PLA may soften over time.
